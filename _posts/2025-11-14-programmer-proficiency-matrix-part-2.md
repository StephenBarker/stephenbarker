    <p class="mt-2">The transmission continues. Last cycle, we decoded the hard skills from the AssetCorp Global© proficiency matrix. Today, we dissect the part HR insists matters more than your codebase: the Human OS.</p>

    <h3 class="crt-green mt-2">Soft Skills: The Human OS</h3>

    <h4 class="crt-green mt-2">Communication</h4>
    <ul class="list--custom">
        <li><strong>Level 1:</strong> Grunts in Jira tickets. Communicates primarily through code comments and commit messages.</li>
        <li><strong>Level 2:</strong> Can explain technical concepts to non-technical stakeholders without causing panic.</li>
        <li><strong>Level 3:</strong> Convinces executives they are "aligned with strategic vision" while secretly building what's actually needed.</li>
    </ul>

    <h4 class="crt-green mt-2">Collaboration</h4>
    <ul class="list--custom">
        <li><strong>Level 1:</strong> Works with others without rage-quitting Slack.</li>
        <li><strong>Level 2:</strong> Practices diplomacy in code reviews, offering constructive feedback instead of just pointing out flaws.</li>
        <li><strong>Level 3:</strong> Fosters a collaborative environment, mentors junior devs, and turns team friction into productive debate.</li>
    </ul>

    <h4 class="crt-green mt-2">Problem Solving</h4>
    <ul class="list--custom">
        <li><strong>Level 1:</strong> Fixes typos and simple bugs.</li>
        <li><strong>Level 2:</strong> Debugs complex production issues under pressure.</li>
        <li><strong>Level 3:</strong> Solves existential architectural crises and anticipates problems before they manifest.</li>
    </ul>

    <h4 class="crt-green mt-2">Time Management</h4>
    <ul class="list--custom">
        <li><strong>Level 1:</strong> Manages a personal task list.</li>
        <li><strong>Level 2:</strong> Balances multiple competing priorities and communicates timeline risks.</li>
        <li><strong>Level 3:</strong> Estimates effort for large projects with something resembling accuracy and helps the team prioritize.</li>
    </ul>

    <h4 class="crt-green mt-2">Adaptability</h4>
    <ul class="list--custom">
        <li><strong>Level 1:</strong> Learns a new framework or tool when required.</li>
        <li><strong>Level 2:</strong> Pivots quickly in response to changing requirements or new mandates.</li>
        <li><strong>Level 3:</strong> Thrives in ambiguity, embraces change, and helps guide the team through transitions.</li>
    </ul>

    <h4 class="crt-green mt-2">Ownership / Accountability</h4>
    <ul class="list--custom">
        <li><strong>Level 1:</strong> Owns their commits.</li>
        <li><strong>Level 2:</strong> Owns their feature from development through deployment and support.</li>
        <li><strong>Level 3:</strong> Owns the outcome of the project, including the fallout from failures.</li>
    </ul>

    <h4 class="crt-green mt-2">Critical Thinking</h4>
    <ul class="list--custom">
        <li><strong>Level 1:</strong> Follows established patterns and best practices.</li>
        <li><strong>Level 2:</strong> Questions existing patterns and proposes improvements.</li>
        <li><strong>Level 3:</strong> Invents new patterns that make other people uncomfortable but ultimately prove superior.</li>
    </ul>

    <p class="mt-2">The final transmission will cover the skills that actually pay the bills. Don't miss it.</p>
