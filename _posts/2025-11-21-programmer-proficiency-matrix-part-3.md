    <h3 class="crt-green mt-2">Domain Specific Skills: Because Business Still Pays the Bills</h3>

    <h4 class="crt-green mt-2">Financial Systems Knowledge</h4>
    <ul class="list--custom">
        <li><strong>Level 1:</strong> Knows a GL from a glorified spreadsheet.</li>
        <li><strong>Level 2:</strong> Understands how sub-ledgers feed the GL and can debug discrepancies.</li>
        <li><strong>Level 3:</strong> Designs financial data models and integrations that auditors praise for their clarity.</li>
    </ul>

    <h4 class="crt-green mt-2">HR/Payroll Processes</h4>
    <ul class="list--custom">
        <li><strong>Level 1:</strong> Understands why payroll errors make enemies.</li>
        <li><strong>Level 2:</strong> Can trace payroll calculations and benefit deductions through the system.</li>
        <li><strong>Level 3:</strong> Architects HR data flows and automates compliance reporting for ACA, EEO, etc.</li>
    </ul>

    <h4 class="crt-green mt-2">Vendor Management</h4>
    <ul class="list--custom">
        <li><strong>Level 1:</strong> Prevents duplicate vendors from breeding like data rabbits.</li>
        <li><strong>Level 2:</strong> Builds logic to enforce vendor validation and payment terms.</li>
        <li><strong>Level 3:</strong> Integrates vendor portals and automates the procure-to-pay lifecycle.</li>
    </ul>

    <h4 class="crt-green mt-2">Data Governance/Compliance</h4>
    <ul class="list--custom">
        <li><strong>Level 1:</strong> Writes code that won’t summon auditors.</li>
        <li><strong>Level 2:</strong> Implements data retention policies and access controls.</li>
        <li><strong>Level 3:</strong> Designs and enforces a data governance framework, becoming the person auditors call first.</li>
    </ul>

    <h4 class="crt-green mt-2">ERP Systems / BusinessPlus</h4>

        <li><strong>Level 3:</strong> Can extend the ERP with custom modules and APIs that are more stable than the core product.</li>
    </ul>
